Grand Child Empowerment

Yes, you read that right. I am a grandchild to one Wilkister Odero, a woman with a pure soul and a disciplinarian to the core. She is the only grandparent I know since two of them died before I was conceived and one just before I was old enough to know him and so we love her to bits and of cause visit her regularly. She has brought up her children, our parents, well and I can proudly say she is enjoying the harvest in her old age. Her fridge is always full with all the delicacies, to be honest that is one of the reasons I visit her, to help her eat since she literally has no teeth - her words not mine. This one time we visited her and as we prepared to get back she called me aside to her room. I knew what she was calling me for because it was a norm, this was a time you could go to collect gifts to carry home ranging from shoes, vitenge material , body oils to even some good cash. But this time it was different I was given a ‘lecture’ before the goodies came forth. “Nya ‘Wanda,” she said “usikimbilie bwana kama hauna kazi alafu bwana akuache hapo uangaikee, pata kitu yako kidogo kwanza ndio uolewee. Iwinjo!” then she handed me 1000 shillings. In direct translation she said “Nya ‘Wanda, don’t run to get married if you don’t have a job and then your husband leaves you suffering, get something little for yourself first before you get married. Am I clear?” I did not take it that serious thinking to myself, she is not of our times. But it really struck me when she repeated it when we were bidding her goodbye. I know that statement is very debatable in our society and even in our churches. It is believed that the man is the provider and the wife should stay at home and take care of the children. I understand where such people are coming from and am not writing this to dispute that. But am writing this to encourage the ladies out there to do something with their lives while they are taking care of their children and even before they get the children. In the past, to be a wife you were to attain a certain age and also be a master of simple house chores, or else you will go back to your mother for retraining. But times have changed. There is a song by the Tanzanian_women_all_stars called Superwoman, in one of the verses they say, “Mwanamke ngangari, mwanamke hodari, anayejiamini baba alete chumvi, yeye aletee sukari, mwanamke si tu vipodozi, na vipicha vya kichokozi. Sikuizi mwanamume anapenda mwanamke ajitume na achape kazi.” Translated this means, ‘A firm and great woman, one that believes in herself to bring sugar when the husband brings salt. A woman is not only makeup and taking disturbing photos. Nowadays a man loves a woman who is self-motivated and works with her own hands.’ This is very true, in this current age a man will value a woman who can help him provide for the family. In the past five decades or so, women empowerment has been a top notch global rally, so much at the expense of the boy child. The girl child has been given priority and affirmed in the corporate world and even in other sectors of employment. All that a lady needs to do is to rise up to this levels of professionalism. But there is a problem, we often overlook. This should challenge us to work hard so to earn this chances. It will be so sad when time comes for the boy child to be empowered and yet as a woman you have done nothing to improve yourself. Let us be proactive and grasp such opportunities before they become too scarce. Regardless of how young you are, have a vision for your life. Even though that is not where you’ll probably end up, but it keeps you grounded. Value your life as a woman and do not put all your hope on a man to provide for you and take care of you. That is laziness. It then brings the question of why you had to go to school at all, why did your parents educate you. Work hard and achieve something for yourself. This does not necessarily mean to do something that is just book-oriented, there are many other things that one can do with herself. Acquire skills to improve yourself no matter how mundane it may be; knitting, cooking, baking, sewing and so on, then practice it to perfection. As they say ‘sweep so well that people will say there once lived a great sweeper’. In conclusion the bible in Proverbs 31:15-23 says; She gets up while it is still night; she provides food for her family and portions for her female servants. She considers a field and buys it out of her earnings she plants a vineyard. She sets about her work vigorously; her arms are strong for her tasks. She sees that her trading is profitable and her lamp does not go out at night. In her hand she grasps the spindle with her fingers. She opens her arms to the poor and extends her hands to the needy. When it snows, she has no fear for her household for all of them are clothed in scarlet. She makes coverings for her bed; she is clothed in fine linen and purple. Her husband is respected I the city gate where he takes his seat among the elders of the land. She makes linen garments and sells them and supplies the merchants with sashes. She is clothed with strength and dignity; she can laughs at the days to come. May we ladies therefore seek to be this kind of a woman.
